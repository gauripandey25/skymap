Spica

Details

  • Name: Spica
  • Type: Binary star system
  • Location: Constellation Virgo
  • Magnitude: 0.98 (Apparent)
  • Distance: Approximately 260 light-years from Earth
  • Notable Features: Blue giant with a companion star

History

Spica has been used in navigation and has been an important star in various cultures. It is also used as a reference point for the position of the Galactic Plane.

Legends

In Roman mythology, Spica is associated with the goddess of grain, and in Greek mythology, it is linked to the goddess Demeter. The star is often depicted as a spike of wheat.

Interesting Facts

  • Spica is one of the closest blue giant stars to Earth.
  • The star’s rapid rotation causes it to be significantly flattened at the poles.
  • Spica’s companion star is also a blue giant and is located at a close distance.
