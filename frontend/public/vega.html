Vega

Details

  • Name: Vega
  • Type: Main-sequence star
  • Location: Constellation Lyra
  • Magnitude: 0.03 (Apparent)
  • Distance: Approximately 25 light-years from Earth
  • Notable Features: Rapid rotation, blue-white color

History

Vega has played a significant role in navigation and astronomy throughout history. Its name derives from the Arabic word "Waqi," meaning "falling" or "swooping."

Legends

Vega has inspired many legends across cultures, including its association with Orpheus's lyre in Greek mythology and "Zhi Nu," the Weaving Maid, in Chinese astronomy.

Interesting Facts

  • Vega has been extensively studied in the infrared spectrum.
  • It was the North Star about 12,000 years ago and will be again in about 13,000 years.
  • Its rapid rotation causes a noticeable equatorial bulge.
  • It is the fifth-brightest star in the night sky and the second-brightest in the northern hemisphere.
