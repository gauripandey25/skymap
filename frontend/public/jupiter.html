Jupiter

Details

  • Name: Jupiter
  • Type: Gas giant
  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Orbit: 11.86 Earth years
  • Distance from Sun: 778.3 million km
  • Notable Features: Great Red Spot, strong magnetic field, numerous moons

History

Jupiter has been known since ancient times and was named after the king of the Roman gods. Its massive size and prominent features have fascinated astronomers for centuries.

Legends

In mythology, Jupiter (or Zeus in Greek mythology) was the king of the gods, representing authority and power. The planet's size and prominence reflected its godly status.

Interesting Facts

  • Jupiter has a strong magnetic field and radiation belts.
  • It has at least 79 moons, including the four largest, known as the Galilean moons.
  • The Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has been raging for at least 400 years.
