Antares

Details

  • Name: Antares
  • Type: Red supergiant star
  • Location: Constellation Scorpius
  • Magnitude: 1.06 (Apparent)
  • Distance: Approximately 550 light-years from Earth
  • Notable Features: Its reddish hue and immense size

History

Antares has been a significant star in various cultures, often referred to as the "heart" of the scorpion in Scorpius. Its name is derived from the Greek word for "rival of Ares," the red planet Mars.

Legends

In Greek mythology, Antares was considered the heart of the scorpion. It has also been a key feature in many ancient star maps and navigational charts.

Interesting Facts

  • Antares is roughly 700 times the diameter of the Sun.
  • It is one of the largest stars visible to the naked eye.
  • Antares is a binary star system, with a smaller companion star orbiting it.
