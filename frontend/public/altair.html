Altair

Details

  • Name: Altair
  • Type: Main-sequence star
  • Location: Constellation Aquila
  • Magnitude: 0.77 (Apparent)
  • Distance: Approximately 16.7 light-years from Earth
  • Notable Features: Rapid rotation, bright blue-white color

History

Altair has been recognized for its brightness and is a significant star in many cultural mythologies. It has been used as a navigational guide throughout history.

Legends

In ancient cultures, Altair was often associated with the eagle or the celestial bird. In Chinese astronomy, it is known as "Yuheng," the "Dipper Star."

Interesting Facts

  • Altair is one of the vertices of the Summer Triangle, along with Vega and Deneb.
  • The star rotates extremely fast, with an equatorial rotation speed of around 286 km/s.
  • Altair's rapid rotation causes it to be noticeably oblate at the poles.
  • It is one of the closest stars to the solar system that is visible to the naked eye.
