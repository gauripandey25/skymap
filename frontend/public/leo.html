Leo

Details

  • Name: Leo
  • Type: Constellation
  • Location: Northern sky
  • Notable Features: The Lion's Head, Regulus, Denebola

History

Leo has been known since antiquity and is associated with the Nemean Lion from Greek mythology, slain by Heracles as one of his twelve labors.

Legends

In Greek mythology, the constellation represents the Nemean Lion, which was a fearsome beast slain by Heracles. The lion's image is represented by the constellation's prominent stars.

Interesting Facts

  • Leo is one of the twelve zodiac constellations and is visible in the evening sky during spring.
  • The constellation's brightest star, Regulus, is often referred to as the "Heart of the Lion."
  • Leo contains several interesting deep-sky objects, including the Leo Triplet of galaxies.
