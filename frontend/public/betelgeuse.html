Betelgeuse

Details

  • Name: Betelgeuse
  • Type: Red supergiant
  • Location: Constellation Orion
  • Magnitude: 0.42 (Apparent)
  • Distance: Approximately 640 light-years from Earth
  • Notable Features: Variable star, red color

History

Betelgeuse has been a prominent feature of the night sky for centuries and has been the subject of various astronomical studies due to its variability and size.

Legends

In ancient times, Betelgeuse was often depicted as a warrior's shoulder or arm in various mythologies and constellations, adding to its significance in the night sky.

Interesting Facts

  • Betelgeuse is one of the largest known stars, with a diameter estimated to be over 1,000 times that of the Sun.
  • The star is nearing the end of its life and may eventually go supernova.
  • Its distinctive red color is due to its low surface temperature compared to other stars.
  • Betelgeuse's brightness varies significantly over time, making it a classic example of a variable star.
