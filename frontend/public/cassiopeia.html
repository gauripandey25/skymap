Cassiopeia

Details

  • Name: Cassiopeia
  • Type: Constellation
  • Location: Northern sky
  • Notable Features: The W shape, Alpha Cassiopeiae (Schedar), and the open cluster Messier 103

History

Cassiopeia is named after a queen from Greek mythology who was known for her vanity. The constellation has been known since ancient times and is visible throughout the year in northern latitudes.

Legends

In mythology, Cassiopeia was a queen who boasted about her beauty, leading to her being placed among the stars as a punishment. Her constellation is often depicted as a seated queen in a chair.

Interesting Facts

  • Cassiopeia is one of the 88 modern constellations and is easily recognizable due to its distinctive W shape.
  • The constellation contains several deep-sky objects, including the open cluster Messier 103 and the variable star R Cassiopeiae.
  • It is located opposite the constellation Ursa Major in the sky, making it visible at different times of the year.
