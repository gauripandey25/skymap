Pavo

Details

  • Name: Pavo
  • Type: Constellation
  • Location: Southern sky
  • Notable Features: Alpha Pavonis, the Peacock Nebula

History

Pavo is a relatively modern constellation introduced by the Dutch astronomer Pieter Dirkszoon in the 17th century. It represents the peacock, a symbol of beauty and grace.

Legends

The peacock in mythology is often associated with the goddess Hera in Greek mythology. Its inclusion in the night sky highlights its splendor and significance.

Interesting Facts

  • Alpha Pavonis, the brightest star in Pavo, is also known as Peacock's eye.
  • The constellation contains several notable deep-sky objects, including the Peacock Nebula (NGC 6751).
  • Pavo is best viewed from the southern hemisphere and is not visible from most northern latitudes.
