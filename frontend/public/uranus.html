Uranus

Details

  • Name: Uranus
  • Type: Ice giant
  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Orbit: 84 Earth years
  • Distance from Sun: 2.871 billion km
  • Notable Features: Extreme axial tilt, faint ring system, large moons

History

Uranus was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel. It is named after the Greek deity of the sky, reflecting its distant and mysterious nature.

Legends

In mythology, Uranus was the primordial sky god, representing the heavens and the boundary between earth and sky. The planet's unique tilt symbolizes a break from conventional patterns.

Interesting Facts

  • Uranus rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees.
  • It has a faint ring system, discovered in 1977.
  • Uranus has 27 known moons, many of which are named after characters from Shakespeare's plays.
