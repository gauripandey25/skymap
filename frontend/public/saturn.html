Saturn

Details

  • Name: Saturn
  • Type: Gas giant
  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Orbit: 29.46 Earth years
  • Distance from Sun: 1.429 billion km
  • Notable Features: Extensive ring system, numerous moons

History

Saturn has been observed since ancient times and was named after the Roman god of agriculture. Its distinctive rings make it one of the most recognizable planets in the Solar System.

Legends

In mythology, Saturn (or Cronus in Greek mythology) was the god of time and harvest. The planet's rings symbolize the cycles of time and the passing of seasons.

Interesting Facts

  • Saturn's rings are made primarily of ice particles with a small amount of rock.
  • It has at least 83 moons, including Titan, which is larger than the planet Mercury.
  • Saturn's atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with trace amounts of other gases.
