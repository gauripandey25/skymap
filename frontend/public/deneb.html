Deneb

Details

  • Name: Deneb
  • Type: Supergiant star
  • Location: Constellation Cygnus
  • Magnitude: 1.25 (Apparent)
  • Distance: Approximately 1,425 light-years from Earth
  • Notable Features: One of the most distant stars visible to the naked eye

History

Deneb has been a prominent star in various cultures and has been used in astronomical navigation. Its name is derived from the Arabic word "Dhanab," meaning "tail."

Legends

In Greek mythology, Deneb is associated with the tail of the swan in the constellation Cygnus, representing the swan's flight across the sky.

Interesting Facts

  • Deneb is one of the vertices of the Summer Triangle, along with Vega and Altair.
  • It is one of the most luminous stars known, with a luminosity about 200,000 times that of the Sun.
  • Deneb's light takes approximately 1,425 years to reach Earth.
