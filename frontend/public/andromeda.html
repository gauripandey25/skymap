Andromeda

Details

  • Name: Andromeda
  • Type: Constellation
  • Location: Northern sky
  • Notable Features: Andromeda Galaxy (M31), Alpha Andromedae (Alpheratz)

History

Andromeda has been known since ancient times and is associated with the Greek mythological figure Andromeda, who was chained to a rock as a sacrifice to a sea monster.

Legends

According to Greek mythology, Andromeda was saved by Perseus, who later married her. The constellation represents her chained position as she awaited her rescue.

Interesting Facts

  • The Andromeda Galaxy is the nearest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way and is on a collision course with our galaxy.
  • Andromeda is one of the 88 modern constellations and is visible in the northern hemisphere throughout the year.
  • It contains several notable stars and deep-sky objects, making it a popular target for amateur astronomers.
