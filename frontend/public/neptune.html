Neptune

Details

  • Name: Neptune
  • Type: Ice giant
  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Orbit: 164.8 Earth years
  • Distance from Sun: 4.495 billion km
  • Notable Features: Strong winds, Great Dark Spot, deep blue color

History

Neptune was discovered in 1846 by Johann Galle and Heinrich d'Arrest based on mathematical predictions. It was named after the Roman god of the sea due to its deep blue color.

Legends

In mythology, Neptune (or Poseidon in Greek mythology) was the god of the sea. The planet's blue color and mysterious nature reflect the vast and deep oceans of the mythological Neptune.

Interesting Facts

  • Neptune has the strongest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds up to 2,100 km/h.
  • It has a large moon named Triton, which orbits Neptune in the opposite direction of the planet's rotation.
  • The planet's deep blue color comes from the presence of methane in its atmosphere.
